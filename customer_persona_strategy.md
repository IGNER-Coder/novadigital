# Digital Marketing Customer Persona & Positioning Strategy

This document outlines the primary customer persona for a new digital marketer in Kenya and a step-by-step strategy to attract and serve them.

---

### Your Primary Customer Persona: "The Ambitious SME Owner"

Let's give this persona a name: **"Wanjiku, The Entrepreneur."**

*   **Who is she?** Wanjiku is the founder and owner of a Small or Medium-sized Enterprise (SME) in Kenya. Her business is 2-7 years old, has a small team (3-15 employees), and is her primary focus.
*   **Her Business:** It could be a retail shop in Nairobi (e.g., a boutique, electronics store), a professional service provider (e.g., a law firm, an accounting practice), a restaurant, a small-scale manufacturer, or a tour operator. The business is successful enough to be stable but needs to grow to the next level.
*   **Her Digital Savviness:** She is a regular user of WhatsApp, Facebook, and Instagram for personal use. She has created a Facebook or Instagram page for her business but isn't sure what to do with it beyond posting occasional pictures. She hears terms like "SEO," "Google Ads," and "content marketing" but feels overwhelmed and doesn't know where to start.
*   **Her Goal:** To increase sales, get more customers, and build a reputable brand that can compete with larger businesses.

---

### Wanjiku's Pain Points (What Keeps Her Up at Night)

| Pain Point Category | Specific Frustrations & Worries |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Overwhelm & Lack of Time** | "I'm the CEO, accountant, and head of sales. I don't have time to learn and manage social media, let alone something called SEO." |
| **2. Invisible Online** | "My competitors are all over Instagram and Google. When people search for 'best wedding planner in Nairobi,' they show up, but my business is nowhere to be found. I feel invisible." |
| **3. Wasted Money & No ROI** | "I've tried 'boosting' posts on Facebook, but all I get are likes from my cousins. It doesn't lead to actual sales. I feel like I'm just burning money with no return on investment (ROI)." |
| **4. Confusion & Lack of Clarity** | "Should I be on TikTok? Do I need a website? What's the difference between Google Ads and SEO? I don't know what will actually work for *my* business." |
| **5. Fear of Being Left Behind** | "The world is moving online. If I don't figure this out soon, my business will be left behind. My traditional marketing methods (flyers, word-of-mouth) are not as effective anymore." |
| **6. Trust & Finding the Right Help** | "I can't afford a big, expensive agency. I'm worried about hiring a 'guru' who will make big promises, take my money, and deliver nothing. I need a reliable partner I can trust." |

---

### Your Positioning Strategy: How to Become Wanjiku's Solution

Here is a step-by-step procedure to position yourself as the go-to resource for this persona.

| Step | Action | How It Solves Wanjiku's Pain Points |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Build Your Professional Showcase** | **Create a simple, professional online presence for yourself.** This includes a clean LinkedIn profile, a professional Facebook/Instagram page for your brand, and ideally, a simple one-page website. **Showcase results, not just services.** (e.g., "Helped a local boutique increase foot traffic by 30% through targeted Instagram ads"). | **Solves Trust:** It shows you are a serious professional, not a random person. It demonstrates you understand the very services you plan to offer. |
| **2. Become the "De-mystifier"** | **Create simple, jargon-free content.** Post short videos or carousels on your social media explaining basic concepts. **Examples:** "What is SEO? Think of it as making your business the first result on Google Maps." or "3 Simple Ways a Restaurant Can Use Instagram Stories to Get More Lunch Orders." | **Solves Confusion & Overwhelm:** You are giving her clarity and value for free. You are translating complex ideas into simple, actionable business advice, building trust and authority. |
| **3. Focus on Results, Not Jargon** | **Change your language.** Instead of selling "SEO Services," sell "**Get Found on Google by More Customers**." Instead of "Social Media Management," sell "**Turn Your Social Media Fans into Paying Customers**." | **Solves Wasted Money & No ROI:** This speaks directly to her goals. She doesn't care about "engagement rates"; she cares about sales. This shows you are focused on her bottom line. |
| **4. Create "SME-Friendly" Packages** | **Design clear, affordable, entry-level packages.** For example: **"Social Media Starter Pack (KES 20,000/month):** We manage your Facebook & Instagram, post 3 times a week, and provide a monthly report on leads generated." | **Solves Trust & Wasted Money:** This makes your services accessible and transparent. She knows exactly what she's getting and for how much, reducing her fear of being overcharged.. |
| **5. Network Where Wanjiku Is** | **Engage in local business communities.** Join Kenyan SME groups on Facebook and LinkedIn. Attend local business networking events (e.g., those hosted by chambers of commerce). Don't sell; just offer helpful advice in the comments. | **Solves Invisibility (for you) & Trust:** This is how you find your clients. By being a helpful voice in her community, you build trust and become the person she thinks of when she's ready to hire. |
| **6. Offer a "Foot-in-the-Door" Service** | **Provide a free, high-value consultation or audit.** Offer a "Free 30-Minute Digital Strategy Session" or a "Free Social Media Page Audit." In this session, provide genuine advice and point out 2-3 things she could improve immediately. | **Solves Trust & Confusion:** This is the ultimate trust-builder. You provide immense value upfront with no strings attached, proving your expertise and building a relationship before asking for a sale. |
| **7. Be Both Remote and On-Site** | **Position yourself as a local partner.** Emphasize that you are based in Kenya and understand the local market. Offer to have an initial meeting on-site at her business premises to understand her operations, then manage the work remotely. | **Solves Trust:** Being able to meet in person, even just once, builds a huge amount of trust compared to a completely faceless international provider. It shows you are invested in her local business. |
